About Me

Mathematics as a
universal language

I'm a mathematician, modeler, and data scientist who believes that almost any complex system — whether a financial market, a physical process, a biological network, or an AI pipeline — can be understood and optimised through the right mathematical framework.

Currently working as a Quantitative Developer (AVP) on the FX Quant Team at Wells Fargo, where I design stochastic volatility models, ML-driven risk frameworks, and backtesting pipelines for derivatives and FX markets.

My toolkit spans Bayesian inference, Monte Carlo simulation, stochastic processes, reinforcement learning, and quantum computing. I'm drawn to hard problems — the kind that require building models from scratch and validating them rigorously against real data.

Beyond finance, I'm actively exploring biotechnology, disease modelling, and AI — fields where the same mathematical rigour opens entirely new frontiers. This site is my living notebook of work, ideas, and things I find worth sharing.
